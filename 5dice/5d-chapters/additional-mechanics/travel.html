Travel

Whenever characters attempt to traverse a distance across a landscape that generally does not provide enough visual interest to warrant moment-to-moment roleplay, the narrator may choose to divert to the travel event system. This system offers a simplified way of handling travel by compressing the journey into a montage of events that players get to encounter and play out.

Defining a jouney

To use this travel event system, the narrator needs to define the distance that is to be traversed and categorize it on three main distances: Near, Far and Very Far. The exact distance is not relevant to decide on the systemic distance, but they are exponentally incremental of another. The relative distance dictates how many events are going to be presented on this journey:

  • Near is the shortest distance, only 1 event gets added to the journey.
  • Far is the next longer distance, it has 2 events added to the journey.
  • Very far is the farthest distance that can be traveled without any rest in between, 3 events get added to the journey in this case.
    The narrator should be aware of how far the group has traveled since the last rest to suggest when the party takes a rest for the day.
Tip

The party needing rest can be pinned on specific characters having a lower endurance or being of a lineage with below average physical abilities, but this option should be considered with a lot of care to not stir up conflicts between the players.

Planning the journey

Planning the journey could also be presented as an optimization problem, where the goal is to get to a specific location within a certain time frame while using up as little resources as possible.
This could lead to characters researching possible paths and dangers along these, stocking up supplies and tools, and looking for options to potentially hitch a ride to boost their exploration speed.

Example

A group of adventurers starts off their journey in a small town in the east of the country. They find that their path leads them to the west, into the central capitol. The narrator grants the parties historian knowledge about their potential paths, one option leads through the swamp, where after an improvised camp they will be able to make a stop at a village if they manage to convince the locals they have no ill intentions. Another option has them pass through the mountains, where they will have no option to make a stop in an established settlement, but in turn they might be able to shave off a day of travel time. It is now up to the characters to debate their journey path and prepare for heading out.

Playing out the journey

Each of these events acts like a small stop between segments of uninterrupted motion. Both during and outside these events, the roleplay doesnt have to be sidelined, all players are welcome to roleplay as their characters to enhance the scene or provide character building. Just like the rest of the game, this is highly specific to the story and narrative at hand.
