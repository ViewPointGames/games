Glossary

Action

An umbrella-term for a number of unique active behaviors that directly influence the current Environment and Scene.

Arc

Multiple of these form a or story. Can typically be divided into multiple Chapters.

Approach

A special form of rolling a Skill Check, where the Character swaps out one of the required Skill Dice with another they might be better at, as long as the Player can justify this switch narratively.

Chapter

Multiple of these form a narrative Arc. Can typically be divided into multiple Scenes.

Character

A partaker within the Narrative of the game, who is able to interact with their surroundings through Actions.

Complication

A circumstance that alone might be easy to deal with, but rises the tension of the Narrative in the short term.

Dice

A polymetrically shaped body with differently marked faces.

D4

A die with 4 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D6

A die with 6 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D8

A die with 8 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D10

A die with 10 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D12

A die with 12 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D20

A die with 20 consecutively numbered sides, starting at 1.

D%

A D10 but the numbers are multiplied by 10 each.

Dice Pool

Multiple Dice together create a dice pool, which can be used to give more control over a Dice Roll than a single die.

Dice Roll

The act of using one or more dice of certain Magnitudes to determine a randomly generated number called the Result.

Difficulty

A numerical value depicting how hard a task is for an average person of a certain Lineage is to complete.

Injury

A physical harm that is inflicted upon a Character. Comes in a severity tier and has to be healed to recover from.

Lineage

A group of offsprings from a common progenitor, who are usually identified by their Mechanical traits.

Magnitude

The number of sides of a die. The commonly used ones are the D4, D6, D8, D10, D12 and D20.

Mechanic

A set of rules that link together to form a unit of gameplay.

Modifier

See either Static Modifier or Momentum.

Momentum

See either Surge Momentum or Stall Momentum.

Narrative

The overarching path through a series of events that occur within the game.

Narrator

The player who describes the World and has it react to the Players actions.

Outcome

The immediate change in the Scene after a Dice Roll turned out a certain Result.

Player

A person playing the game.

Player Character

A character that is controlled by a Player who is not the Narrator.

Result

The number of a Dice Roll, after adding applicable Modifiers.

Complicated Success

A Result of a Skill Check where the sum of the rolled numbers including all modifiers is between 7 and 9.

Critical Failure

A Result of a Skill Check where the sum of the rolled numbers including all modifiers is 3 or less.

Critical Success

A Result of a Skill Check where the sum of the rolled numbers including all modifiers is 15 or above.

Simple Failure

A Result of a Skill Check where the sum of the rolled numbers including all modifiers is between 4 and 6.

Simple Success

A Result of a Skill Check where the sum of the rolled numbers including all modifiers is between 10 and 15.

Role

The job of a Player within a game. Typically is either Narrator or Player.

Roleplaying

The act of embodying a character, fictional or otherwise, through manner of talking, describing their actions, or simply reacting in specific ways.

Progress Clock

A circular disk divided into segments that track results of actions for an abstract goal, where Characters can cooperate on gathering enough successes to accomplish the Clock and each segment takes up a certain time frame.

Scene

The smallest unit of a depicted narrative. Multiple of these form a Chapter.

Setting

A specific time and geographic location within a narrative, either non-fiction or fiction. Can be used interchangeably with World.

Skill

A relative numerical value indicating the training of a Character within a certain domain of Actions. Skills can have values from -1 to 3.

Charms

The ability of a Character to be charming, charismatic, or to read the intentions of others.

Grace

The ability of a Character to perform dextrous tasks that require nimbleness.

Link

The ability of a Character to help and support others.

Might

Raw power.

Wits

The ability of a Character to comprehend complex topics, deduce facts, inventing new tools, or holding general knowledge.

Skill Check

The act of rolling one or two Dice according the the tasks Difficulty, applying any relevant Modifiers and comparing the Result number to generate an Outcome.
Requires a Skill and a Difficulty to be performed by a Player.

Skill Level

The numeric named value a characters skill is at. The 5 skill levels are Inept, Novice, Journeyman, Expert, and Master.

Skill Point

Allows increase of a Skill level of a Character to increase the likelyhood of success on tasks using this Skill.

Stall

Performing a Dice Roll twice and taking the lower Result.

Static Modifier

A number that gets added to the Result of a Dice Roll, creating a new Result. Each Static Modifier can only be added once per Roll.

Surge

Performing a Dice Roll twice and taking the higher Result.

Story

The heart of any game of 5D. Typically split into multiple Arcs.

Subsystem

A set of closely related Mechanics that link together to form a part of a playable game.

System

A set of Subsystems and Mechanics that link together at different interfacing areas to create a complete game.

World

The location a specific game takes place in.
