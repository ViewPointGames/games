Character Sheet

Characters in 5D consist of a multitude of facettes. All of them fall into either of two categories, which are the gameplay properties and the narrative properties.

Narrative

On a narrative level, each character has an identity and a characterization of this person. The identity and characterization form the backbone of how a character acts and views the world around them in any given situation.

Identity

Property Explanation
Name How the character gets referred to by others
Age How long this character has been roaming earth
Origin Where that character has been born
Characteristics Mechanically not significant details to the character, ie changes in looks, behavioral ticks, etc

The identity of a character consists of statistical data to describe measurable information about this character.
This consists of the name and age of the character, as well as their origin and characteristics.
The origin is a characters home place. It tells where the character was born, what kind of life they likely have lived, and pushes a probable belief system onto the character, which is based on the society they are a part of.
The characteristics are a list of detail facts about a character. They can range from noticeable appearance changes, to behavioral ticks. These characteristics do not have any mechanical impact on the gameplay, they serve as roleplay hooks to make the character more interesting.

Characterization

Facette Related question
Backstory How did the character come to be who and where they are now?
Goals What is the characters current dream they wish to achieve?
Beliefs What does the character believe in? Do they follow a strict moral code of some higher authority or rather make it up as they go along?

The characterization section consists of a characters background story, their goals and aspirations, and their beliefs and morals.
The background story aims to explain briefly why and how that character got to the point they are at at the beginning of the story.
The goals and aspirations inform what a character wants to accomplish in their life. This can be a long term goal like "seeing the entire world", or a number of smaller milestones like "trade with different nationalities salesmen" and "buy my own airship".
The beliefs and morals of a character give guidelines for how a character views the world and how they interact with it. This is influenced a lot by the origin society, as they typically lay a ground work for what a character gets served as facts, and what they are allowed to accept as truth.

Tip

This section is deliberately open-ended. When answering each of these questions, try to be creative, but do not be afraid of writing down simpler and easier to understand answers.

Gameplay

On a gameplay level a character has differently scored abilities. Additionally, every character has one to two vocations they are trained at, which when applicable provide a bonus to a required skill.
Likewise, a character holds an amount of some currency in their purse, holds, wears and carries unique equipment in their hands, holsters and pockets, and has suffered different injuries, each with distinct effects and (dis-)abilities. This section is referred to as the characters Status.

Abilities

Abilities lie at the core of everything the character can do to a certain extend. They inform about the strengths and weaknesses of the character, and what areas of expertise they can excel at.

Skills

Every character has access to five skills. These are:

  • Might (M):
    Might is useful for being able to sustain more bodily harm, displaying athletic talent, or hitting things harder.
  • Agility (A):
    Agility gets used for dodging attacks, piloting a machine, or performing acrobatic feats.
  • Wits (W)):
    Wits represents the characters intelligence. This includes their ability to deduce, being more able to craft objects together, or holding a lot of knowledge in their memory.
  • Charms (C):
    Charms displays the ability to trick and persuade other characters, and to read another characters true intentions.
  • Synergy (S):
    Synergy means the characters ability to be supportive towards others, to provide comfort and be a good person.

These five skills are found in the Skill

Skills are measured in training level. This level ranges from -1 to 3, which depicts a relative scale anchored on an average person of the characters lineage. Depending on the training level, Skill Checks use a different combination of dice to perform:

  • -1 (Inept) = D4
  • 0 (Novice) = D6
  • 1 (Journeyman) = D8
  • 2 (Expert) = D10
  • 3 (Master) = D12
Example

Xypha the druid in training has always valued information above everything else. She considers her mind to be her best asset, and loves to be in the known of anything going on around her. Her player allocated 2 skill points into W and another into C to help her extract more knowledge from other people. The last free skill point goes into Synergy. Since Xypha is not considered to be physically strong, but nimble and agile, she got a -1 in S which added one more point to spend in A.

Professions

A character does something while they are alive. What they decide on doing results in their profession. Professions act as bonuses to use whenever appropriate for Skill Checks. A character can have one or two professions, each with their own bonus:

  • a major profession (mandatory):
    This is their main activity for earning their livelihood so far.
    When applicable in a situation (proposed by the player, approved by the narrator), the major profession grants Surge Momentum.
  • a minor profession (optional):
    This can be a side gig, a former job they are still somewhat knowledgeable in, or a simple hobby they like to tend to in their free time.
    When applicable in a situation (proposed by the player, approved by the narrator), the minor profession grants a Static +2 modifier.

In addition to the direct bonuses applied during Skill Checks, the major profession also informs the characters main means to accumulating Adrenaline.

Example: Picking professions

Xypha is a driud in training. As a part of the cove, she lives with other druids who teach her their ways. Thus, her major profession is "Druid". She does not have a minor profession as learning to be a good druid takes up all her time at the moment.
Her player decides together with the narrator that her way of gaining Adrenaline is through seeing nature get hurt directly.

Status

The character status consists of four main elements:

  • The characters purse, which is used for tracking currencies carried by the character
  • Their inventory, which holds space for various objects they carry with them
  • An endurance list for tracking Injuries and Stress they have endured
  • A numerical tracker for metacurrency known as Adrenaline

Purse

A characters purse is the home of any money they can acquire. It acts as a list of all currencies and their amount a character has on them at a time.

Tip: Stealing the purse

This purse can be stolen from the character by others, but it is highly recommended to always give good options for the robbed character to regain their money in order to not increase player resentment.

Inventory

The inventory of a character represents all the different items they are carrying around. The inventory consists of 12 slots. By default, a character can access the first 4 slots, which are called body slots. This can be expanded by having the character carry containers with them; these additional slots are called container slots.

Characters are able to hold items in their hands. To represent this, each player is given one token for each hand a character has. These tokens can be placed on different slots to represent a character holding this item in their hand. If an item requires two hands to be carried, the player must place two opposing hand tokens on that item slot.

If a character has to switch around what their hands are holding during a Skirmish, the player needs to expend 1 action to move the token(s) to body slot(s), or 2 action points to move the token(s) to container slot(s).

Example: Switching items mid-combat

Gabe the miner has his trusty miners pick always on his back, and not inside a backpack. Now he is facing off against a goblin who surprised him. It is now Gabes turn. As the pick is not inside a container, he may use one action point to pick it up from his back. The second action is used by Gabe through an immediate attack using his pick.

Item sizes

Items that can be picked up and carried around come in different sizes. This size is noted down next to the item name. The item sizes are:

  • Tiny (T): can fit up to X amount of that item inside a single slot (amount depends on the item)
  • Small (S): needs 1 inventory slot
  • Medium (M): needs 2 inventory slots
  • Large (L): needs 3 inventory slots
  • Huge (H): can not be carried around inside an inventory
    When a character picks up an item, the player writes down that item and its size in the inventory. Should the item take up more than one slot, the player marks the required number of slots below that item as full by crossing the slot out.
Example

Barry the Bard likes to carry around his lute. This instrument is classified as a large item, meaning it takes up 3 inventory spaces. Barrys player writes down Barrys lute as the item along with the "L" for the size, and then crosses out the two slots below the item to signal these slots as being taken up. When Barry puts down the lute, the player may erase the item and the crossed out markings taken up by the lute from the inventory.

Containers

Containers can take different forms, but they all come in 2 sizes:

  • Small containers take up 1 inventory slot but expand it by 2 additional slots, up to 12 slots in total. Characters can only carry 2 small containers at a time.
  • Large containers take up 2 inventory slots but expand it by 4 additional slots, up to 12 slots in total. Characters can only carry one large container at a time.
    Items that take up more than one slot can not be split between containers, they have to be placed in full inside one container.
Example

Barry the bard has his lute always on the ready to perform another piece. Usually it is strapped around his chest on a holster, but sometimes he likes to not have his movements obstructed. So, he wants to put his instrument away. As the lute is a large item, it needs 3 inventory slots. This means that it can not be placed in a small container, as those only offer 2 slots each. He thus also is required to carry a large container to put his lute into.

Vitality and Harm

The two types of damage a character can suffer and their vitality are noted down in the Harm list and Vitality tracker.
Any Harm is entered into the list with the data points of the harms name, the severity tier and the hit area (if the Harm is an injury).
The name informs about the type of Harm taken. The severity dictates the minimum token size to be placed on that newly added harm. The hit area and the treatment checkbox mark disabled limbs and indicate any to be treated harm in order to regain the placed vitality token.
As the lineage of a character informs the number of limbs that can be disabled through being injured, the amount of each type of limb (arm, leg, other appendage) is noted above the Harm list.

Adrenaline

Adrenaline

Throughout a game of 5D, player characters accumulate a unique resource known as Adrenaline. This resource can be spent to influence the narrative of the game in small but significant ways.

Adrenaline Level

Every player character has an Adrenaline level. This level sits between 0 and an upper limit, which is the characters progression tier plus 1.
Quickplay characters' Adrenaline level can be 3 at most. Only player controlled characters have an Adrenaline level.
Adrenaline Levels reset to 0 after characters finish a Respite or Reclusion

Activating Adrenaline

Adrenaline can be accumulated throughout the game through different ways. Every player character gets one unique method of gathering Adrenaline, which is decided together with the narrator upon character creation. This method usually is unique to that character.

Example

Clio is a human mercenary. Her player and the narrator decided that her way of accumulating Adrenaline lies within being at the front line of a battle. Thus, the exact event that causes the character to gain adrenaline is decided to be whenever she manages to inflict a Wound or more severe injury to an opponent.

Example

Jason the lumberjack loves riddles, this is one of his characters minor quirks. He has a rather high intelligence for it and even a minor vocation set to solving puzzles. The narrator offers Jasons player the option to grant Adrenaline whenever Jason manages to come up with creative and unique solutions to a problem.

Adrenaline Rush

A player may spend any amount of Adrenaline to create an Adrenaline Rush. The player picks one effect from this list of possible effects and pays spends the required amount of Adrenaline:

  • Sheer Luck (costs 1 Adrenaline, as often as desired): The player may add 1 to a Skill Check before the roll to increase their odds of success.
  • Second Wind (costs 2 Adrenaline, once per Skill Check): The player may reroll a Skill Check with the same dice pool. The player has to use the new result.
  • Flipping the Script (costs 2 Adrenaline, once per scene): The player may retroactively alter the scene in favor of their character in a minor way. The narrator has to approve this change, otherwise the player may choose something else.
  • Back from the dead (costs 4, once per reclusion): The player, after breaching their limits during a skirmish, may rise up once to perform a single turn the next time their faction gets to act with permanent Surge Momentum, after which the character can no longer continue onwards and has to totally recover - the character does not die through this option but has to take a reclusion immediately after using this ability.
    After an Adrenaline Rush the character can not experience another until they have taken a Rest.
Example

Clio the mercenary has accumulated her maximum of 3 Adrenaline. When she enters a dark cavern with no preparation, her player announces to spend 2 Adrenaline points in order to Flip the Script, which gives the player the option to add to the scene that Clio luckily has brought a stack of unused torches which she can light up. The remaining Adrenaline remains on the character sheet. Clio can not experience another Adrenaline Rush until she rests.
