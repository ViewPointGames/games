Downtime

Whether it is a quiet nights rest, a week spent gathering heist options, or a season of laying low after a brutal encounter, downtime is the way characters may heal, grow, plan and advance long term tasks that can not be completed in short time frames.
Downtime represents the low-stakes times between intense moments, where not much story relevant activity can be paragraphed, and instead a summary could be given.

On a mechanical level, downtime can be used to recover constitution and resilience from being injured or stressed.
On a narrative level, downtime can be used to further progress in quests, personal goals, tend to hobbies, or provide character or world building.

Downtime can be taken by any character, in three different tiers: Rests, Respites and Reclusions.
Depending on the chosen downtime period, each character may spend the time to perform some low-stakes activities. Additionally, two passive boons are affecting the player automatically at the end of the downtime:

  1. Recovery: Players get to remove a number of injuries or stress from their character sheet depending on their downtime period:

    • At the end of a scene, players get to remove one Agitation.
    • At the end of a rest, players get to remove one Strain, plus all Agitation and one Panic
    • At the end of a respite, players get to remove all Strain and one Wound, plus all Agitation and Panic and one Burnout.
    • At the end of a reclusion, players get to remove all Strains and Wounds and one Trauma, plus any Stress.
      • If a player decides to remove a trauma that was not treated before taking the reclusion, they permanently lose that Constitution slot.

  2. Refresh: Characters regain access to any spent uses of features that can only be used a certain number of times per that downtime period.

Rest

Rests last at least eight hours or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A rest refers to a character taking a break from an intense activity to recover spent strength and unwind from intense moments. During a rest, a character may choose 2 activities.

Activities:

  • Sleeping: A character takes a nap for 4 hours. The player may choose 1 Strain to remove from their character sheet. This option can be picked multiple times to remove more Strains.
  • Replenishing / Reorganizing equipment: A character may choose to reorganize equipment they or a companion carries around to adjust their capabilities for the future. A character can reorganize equipment for multiple characters if they let them, but the active character gets to choose how anyone they reorganize the gear of will have their gear sorted out.
  • Tending to a companion: A character with a medical vocation may tend to another injured character to apply medical treatment of their vocation. The attended character may have one Strian removed from their character sheet.
  • Additional activities: A character may choose to perform other light activity that is fitting for the narrative and the character. This can be highly character and story specific, thus it is crucial to work with the narrator to decide what activities are allowed to be performed

Respite

Respites last at least a week or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A respite refers to a character using multiple days to advance small goals, or to recover from a fierce fight. During a respite, a character may choose 1 activity per day in respite.

Activities:

  • Gathering intel: A character spends the time to collect information about something in low-risk environments.
  • Taking a side hustle: A character picks up a new job to earn some money on the side. The actual amount depends on the type of job and the characters abilities.
  • Errand running: A character runs a sequence of small errands to either earn a tiny amount of money, get information, explore their surroundings, or engage in trading.
  • Acquiring new equipment: A character visits a store or shop to purchase or bargain for new equipment to take it with them on the next journey.
  • Tending to a companion: A character with a medical vocation may tend to another injured character to apply medical treatment of their vocation. The attended character may have one Wound removed from their character sheet.
  • Any Rest Activity

Reclusion

Reclusions last at least 2 months or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A reclusion refers to a character laying low for a long time period without having anything with big stakes happen. During a respite, a character may use all passive boons and choose additionally 1 activity per 2 weeks in reclusion.

Activities:

  • Respecialize: A player may spend any newly earned skill points. Additionally, a player may also remove one skill point from any skill that has at least 0 skill points to spend it in another skill that has at most 2 skill points
  • Training: A player may change their characters vocations in a way fitting with the characters recent experiences
  • Advance a long term goal: A character may take a lot of time to progress a personal quest or task by a significant amount
  • Business Building: A character may use their current vocations to start or expand a business for the time of the reclusion. The amount of money earned depends on the type and location of the business
  • Any Respite Activity

The world keeps turning

While characters are taking time off to recover, the world they inhabit does not stand still. Politics advance, cities bloom and blight, wars are fought, and every time characters decide to take downtime, they lose the ability to largely influence the status of the world.
