Downtime

Whether it is a quiet nights rest, a week spent working for an inn, or a season of laying low after a brutal encounter, downtime is the easiest way characters may heal, grow, plan and advance long term tasks that can not be completed in short time frames.
Downtime represents the low-stakes times between intense moments, where not much story relevant activity can be paragraphed, and instead a summary could be given.

On a mechanical level, downtime can be used to recover constitution and resilience from being injured or stressed.
On a narrative level, downtime can be used to further progress in quests, personal goals, tend to hobbies, or provide character or world building.

Downtime can be taken by any character, in three different tiers: Rests, Respites and Reclusions.
Depending on the chosen downtime period, each character may spend the time to perform some low-stakes activities, the boundaries of which are to be discussed with the narrator.
Additionally, two passive boons are affecting the player automatically at the end of the downtime:

  1. Recovery: Players get to remove some amount of injuries and stress from their character sheet depending on their downtime period, regaining any distributed Vitality tokens not yet collected again:

    • At the end of a scene, players get to remove one Agitation.
    • At the end of a rest, players get to remove one Strain, plus all Agitation and one Panic
    • At the end of a respite, players get to remove all Strain and one Wound, plus all Agitation and Panic and one Burnout.
    • At the end of a reclusion, players get to remove all Strains and Wounds and one Trauma, plus any Stress.
      • If a player decides to remove a trauma that was not treated before taking the reclusion, they permanently lose that Constitution slot.

  2. Refresh: Characters regain access to any spent uses of features that can only be activated a certain number of times per that downtime period.

Rest

Rests last at least eight hours or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A rest refers to a character taking a break from an intense activity to recover spent strength and unwind from intense moments. During a rest, a character may choose 2 activities.

Activity examples:

  • Sleeping: A character takes a nap for 6 hours. The player may choose 1 Strain to remove from their character sheet. This option can be picked multiple times to remove more Strains. Choosing this option a second time only increases the time slept to 8 hours instead of 12.
  • Tending to a companion: A character with some medical vocation (or engineering vocation for mechanical beings) may tend to another injured character to apply medical treatment. The tending character rolls a Synergy check using their vocation. A simple success let's the attended player remove one Strain. A critical success let's the attended character remove all Strain they are equipped for. This action costs adequate medical supplies to perform, not having access to enough medical supplies will increase the difficulty of the Skill Check.
  • Providing emotional support: A character may choose to offer support for another struggling character that has at least one Agitation. The supporting character rolls a Synergy check. On a success, the supported character may remove one Panic, on a critical success the supported character may remove all Panic.
  • Additional activities: A character may choose to perform other light activity that is fitting for the narrative and the character. This can be highly character and story specific, thus it is crucial to work with the narrator to decide what activities are allowed to be performed

Respite

Respites last at least a week or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A respite refers to a character using multiple days to advance small goals, or to recover from a fierce fight. During a respite, a character may choose 1 activity per day in respite.

Activity examples:

  • Gathering intel: A character spends the time to collect information about something in a low-risk environments. The narrator may have the player roll one or more mental skill checks depending on hours the character attempts to collect such information, and then attach the info received to the result of the Skill Check. The amount and quality of intel acquired depends on the number of days spent with this activity.
  • Taking a side hustle: A character picks up a new job to earn a decent amount of money on the side. The actual amount depends on the type of job, the characters abilities and the number of days spent with this activity. This may affect the characters Vitality recovery.
  • Errand running: A character runs a sequence of small errands to either earn a tiny amount of money, get information, explore their surroundings, or engage in trading. One errand sequence takes up one day.
  • Acquiring new equipment: A character visits a store or shop to purchase or bargain for new equipment to take it with them on the next journey. This activity takes up a whole day, but may be combined with other shopping sprees into the same activity.
  • Tending to a companion: A character with some medical vocation (or engineering vocation for mechanical beings) may tend to another injured character to apply medical treatment. The tending character rolls a Synergy check using their vocation. A simple success let's the attended player remove one Wound. A critical success let's the attended character remove all Wounds they have, or mark a Trauma as treated. This action costs adequate medical supplies to perform, not having access to enough medical supplies will increase the difficulty of the Skill Check.
  • Providing emotional support: A character may choose to offer support for another struggling character that has at least one Agitation. The supporting character rolls a Synergy check. On a success, the supported character may remove one Burnout, on a critical success the supported character may remove all Stress from their harm list.
  • Any Rest Activity

Reclusion

Reclusions last at least 2 months or an equivalent time frame, unless otherwise stated. A reclusion refers to a character laying low for a long time period without having anything with big stakes happen. During a respite, a character may use all passive boons and choose additionally 1 activity per 2 weeks or an equivalent time frame in reclusion.

Activity examples:

  • Respecialize: A player may spend any newly earned skill points. Additionally, a player may also repurpose one skill point from one skill to another, as long as the resulting skill points stay between -1 and 3.
  • Training: A player may change their characters vocations any way they see fit that match the characters recent experiences.
  • Advance a long term goal: A character may take a lot of time to progress a personal quest or task by a massive amount, potentially providing some outcome to this task. See ../Proben > Progress Clocks for details.
  • Business Building: A character may use their current vocations to start or expand a business for the time of the reclusion. The amount of money earned depends on the type and location of the business
  • Any Respite Activity

The world keeps turning

While characters are taking time off to recover, the world they inhabit does not stand still. Politics advance, cities bloom and blight, wars are fought, and every time characters decide to take downtime, they lose the ability to largely influence the status of the world.
